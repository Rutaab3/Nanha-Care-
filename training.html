Why Training Matters

At NanhaCare, we ensure all our babysitters are thoroughly prepared to provide the highest quality care. Our comprehensive training program covers all essential aspects of childcare:

Safety First

Emergency preparedness, childproofing, and hazard recognition training to keep children safe.

Health & Wellness

Recognizing illness symptoms, proper hygiene, and administering basic first aid.

Development

Age-appropriate activities to support cognitive, physical, and emotional growth.

Emotional Care

Building trust, managing behavior, and creating a nurturing environment.

Training by Age Categories

Our specialized training prepares babysitters to meet the unique needs of each developmental stage.

Infants (0–1 year)

Specialized care for the most delicate stage of development, focusing on safety and nurturing.

  • Safe handling & feeding techniques (bottle and breastfeeding support)
  • Sleep safety & SIDS prevention guidelines
  • Diapering & hygiene best practices
  • Emergency response training (choking, fever, allergic reactions)
  • Recognizing developmental milestones
  • Soothing techniques for colic and fussiness

Toddlers (1–3 years)

Guidance for managing the energetic and exploratory toddler phase.

  • Toilet training assistance techniques
  • Encouraging safe play & motor skill development
  • Managing tantrums and emotional outbursts calmly
  • Nutrition basics (preparing small meals & healthy snacks)
  • Language development activities
  • Setting appropriate boundaries

Preschool (3–5 years)

Supporting early learning and social development in preparation for school.

  • Learning through play (educational games, storytelling)
  • Positive discipline techniques and behavior management
  • Socialization support and playdate supervision
  • Hygiene & routine building (handwashing, teeth brushing)
  • Creative arts and craft activities
  • Pre-literacy and numeracy skills

School Age (5+ years)

Engaging older children while maintaining safety and supporting their independence.

  • Homework help & structured activity planning
  • Healthy screen-time balance strategies
  • Safety awareness (stranger danger, road safety, online safety)
  • Emotional support & communication techniques
  • Conflict resolution between siblings/friends
  • Encouraging responsibility and independence
